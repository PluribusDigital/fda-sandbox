What's This All About?

Staff at STSI developed a prototype primarily based on openFDA data made available by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration. We noticed that there was lots of great data, with meaningful connections between data elements. However, the connections were not apparent or easily accessible.

This prototype is all about those connections. We expose connections in two big ways:

  1. Combining information about any given drug into a comprehensive detail page, including data from openFDA, but also other FDA and external sources.
  2. Exploring connections through a tree visualization tool.

Data Sources Used

This prototype uses and remixes several datasets to provide a more combined, connected view of drug data. The primary data sources used are:

  • openFDA Drug Labeling
  • openFDA Drug Adverse Events
  • openFDA Drug Enforcement
  • Medicaid National Average Drug Acquisition Cost (NADAC)
  • FDA Medication Guides
  • FDA Shortage Data
  • National Drug Code Directory
